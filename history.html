Our History

The journey of bringing humor back into the rigid world of fine art.

2020: The Spark

Gallery Mockers began as a small digital collective. Frustrated by the overly serious tone of traditional art exhibitions, a group of contemporary digital artists started subtly photoshopping modern elements into Renaissance paintings just for fun. What started as an inside joke quickly gained traction online.

Jan 2021: The Viral Outbreak

Our "Mona Lisa scrolling TikTok" piece unexpectedly went viral, reaching 5 million views overnight and crashing our rudimentary portfolio site.

Mar 2021: The First Takedown

Received our very first cease-and-desist from a highly traditional art institution. We framed the letter and hung it in our digital gallery.

May 2021: Rebranding the Mockery

Officially established the name "Gallery Mockers," designing our iconic logo and setting out a concrete vision to satirize classical tropes.

Jul 2021: The 10K Milestone

Hit 10,000 active community members on Discord, transforming our project from a solitary joke into a collaborative digital movement.

Sep 2021: "Pixels & Paint" Concept

Pioneered a new workflow combining classical oil painting techniques with heavy pixelation overlays to comment on internet censorship.

Nov 2021: Print-on-Demand Launch

Began offering high-quality prints of our parodies. Our "Girl with a Pearl Earbud" became an instant bestseller among tech enthusiasts.

Dec 2021: Mocker of the Year

Hosted our first annual digital awards ceremony, crowning the best satirical artist within our growing online community.

Feb 2022: Expanding the Collective

Recruited 10 new permanent artists from 5 different continents, truly globalizing our perspective on art history and humor.

2022: The First Exhibition

We hosted our very first pop-up gallery. Titled "Classics Reimagined," the exhibition featured physical prints of our digital satires. The response was overwhelming. Art critics and casual viewers alike appreciated the breath of fresh air and the underlying commentary on modern society.

Jun 2022: "Pop-Art Renaissance" Drop

Released a 50-piece collection merging Andy Warhol's stylistic repetition with Caravaggio's dramatic lighting.

Aug 2022: Cafe Collaborations

Partnered with indie coffee shops across Southeast Asia to feature our art on takeaway cups, bringing satire to morning commutes.

Oct 2022: Statues in Sneakers

Launched a viral social media campaign photoshopping limited-edition streetwear onto classical Greek and Roman statues.

Dec 2022: Website V2

Overhauled our entire web presence, introducing the smooth, interactive gallery features and animations that define our site today.

Jan 2023: The Singapore Pop-up

Held our first international physical exhibition in Singapore. Tickets sold out in under 48 hours.

Mar 2023: Cover of "Digital Arts"

Featured on the front cover of a major contemporary art magazine, cementing our status as disruptive innovators.

Apr 2023: "Van Gogh's VR Headset"

Our reinterpretation of Van Gogh's self-portrait exploring virtual reality isolation became the most shared art piece of the month.

May 2023: 100K Followers

Surpassed 100,000 followers on Instagram. To celebrate, we gave away 100 custom hand-painted memes.

Jul 2023: Launching AR Mockers

Introduced our Augmented Reality app, allowing users to point their phones at classic paintings in museums to reveal our satirical overlays.

Sep 2023: First Charity Auction

Auctioned off a massive 2x2 meter canvas of "The Last Supper (Delivery)" to raise funds for arts education in underprivileged schools.

Nov 2023: Baroque and Broke

A specialized exhibition focusing on the financial realities of modern artists, depicted through opulent Baroque aesthetics.

Dec 2023: The Year-End Satire Gala

Hosted a formal gala where the dress code required guests to dress as their favorite internet meme painted in a classical style.

Feb 2024: Jakarta Permanent Studio

Opened our first permanent studio and creative hub in Jakarta, serving as the headquarters for our physical operations.

Apr 2024: "Ceci n'est pas un meme"

Paid homage to Magritte with a surrealist digital collection that questioned the very definition of digital ownership and art.

Jun 2024: "The Mocking Bird" Podcast

Launched our official podcast series interviewing artists who blur the line between profound art and absolute absurdity.

Aug 2024: Tokyo Underground Showcase

Brought our collection to Tokyo's thriving underground art scene, integrating cyberpunk elements into our classical satires.

Oct 2024: University Partnerships

Partnered with three major art universities to include digital satire in their contemporary media curriculum.

Dec 2024: Best Digital Collective Award

Won the prestigious "Innovators in Contemporary Art" award at the Asian Digital Art Expo.

Jan 2025: 3D Printed Parodies

Expanded our medium from digital canvas to physical sculptures, utilizing 3D printing to create distorted, satirical classical busts.

Feb 2025: Half a Million Strong

Reached 500,000 followers across all platforms, establishing Gallery Mockers as a mainstream voice in the art world.

Apr 2025: Cubism in the Cubicle

Released a highly relatable series depicting the soul-crushing reality of corporate office culture through Picasso-inspired Cubism.

May 2025: London Calling

Successfully executed a two-week pop-up gallery in London's Soho district, drawing praise from notoriously harsh local critics.

Jul 2025: Interactive VR Gallery

Launched a fully immersive Virtual Reality gallery experience, allowing users worldwide to walk through our exhibits from their living rooms.

Sep 2025: Gallery Mockers Academy

Began offering online courses teaching digital manipulation, art history, and the subtle mechanics of visual humor.

Oct 2025: Gothic Nightmares in Pastel

A Halloween special collection that combined terrifying Gothic architecture and folklore with overly cheerful pastel color palettes.

Nov 2025: Tech Hardware Sponsorship

Secured a major sponsorship deal with a leading graphics tablet manufacturer, outfitting our entire roster of artists with cutting-edge tools.

Dec 2025: Five-Year Retrospective

Published a massive coffee table book celebrating our first five years, featuring behind-the-scenes concepts and artist interviews.

Jan 2026: The AI vs. Human Satire Project

Hosted an experimental exhibit pitting human-drawn parodies against AI-generated ones, ultimately proving that algorithms struggle with irony.

Feb 2026: Romanticism in the Metaverse

Explored the isolation of digital spaces by placing classic Romantic-era lone wanderers into sparse, glitchy virtual landscapes.

Mar 2026: The Bali Retreat

Opened a dedicated creative retreat in Bali for our top contributors to collaborate, brainstorm, and paint in a serene environment.

Apr 2026: One Million Community Members

Hit the legendary milestone of 1 million active followers and newsletter subscribers, solidifying our global influence.

May 2026: Dadaism for Gen Z

Curated a wildly successful show that drew parallels between 1920s Dadaist absurdity and contemporary Gen Z internet humor.

Jun 2026: New York City Debut

Made our official entry into the American art market with a massive showcase in Brooklyn, featuring interactive digital installations.

Jul 2026: Global Creator Fund

Launched a $50,000 grant program to support emerging satirical artists who lack the resources to produce high-quality digital art.

Aug 2026: Surrealism in the Supermarket

An ambitious project transforming everyday grocery store aisles into mind-bending, Dali-esque installations to critique consumerism.

Sep 2026: Sustainable Art Initiatives

Transitioned all our physical prints and packaging to 100% recycled materials, ensuring our mockery of society doesn't harm the planet.

Oct 2026: The Modern Fresco Project

Commissioned our artists to paint large-scale satirical murals in major metropolitan cities, bringing the digital joke to the physical streets.

Nov 2026: Interactive Canvas Integration

Debuted proprietary canvas technology that changes the artwork's satirical elements based on the viewer's facial expressions.

Today: A Global Platform

Now, Gallery Mockers stands as a premier platform for satirical contemporary art. We collaborate with over 25 artistic partners globally, host regular physical and digital exhibitions, and ship our unique reimagined masterpieces to collectors worldwide.
